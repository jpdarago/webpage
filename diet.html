    Diet summary

    This plan is built around a simple idea: keep protein adequate, keep fats controlled, and make enough room for carbohydrates so training performance stays high. For strength training and BJJ, carbs matter a lot because they support energy, recovery, and glycogen replenishment.

    The plan is intentionally simple. Most meals follow the same pattern: one carb source + one protein source + fruit or vegetables + a measured fat source. You do not need fancy foods or complex cooking. Rice, potatoes, oats, chicken, lean beef, tuna, yogurt, bread, tortillas, eggs, zucchini, broccoli, carrot, onion and fruit already cover almost everything.

    The main practical rule is consistency. It is better to repeat simple meals you can actually prepare than to chase variety and stop following the plan after three days.

    Daily targets and priorities

    Protein

    130-150 g/day

    Enough to support muscle gain without wasting calories that could go to carbs.

    Carbohydrates

    250-350 g/day

    Push higher on hard training days or when performance feels flat.

    Fats

    Moderate

    Use measured olive oil and adjust down when using fattier meats.

    Hydration

    1.5-3 L/day

    More if you sweat a lot during BJJ or summer training.

    Practical priority order

    • Hit carbs around training.
    • Keep protein in range, not excessive.
    • Measure added fats instead of pouring "a little bit" from the bottle.
    • Use vegetables mainly for health, satiety, digestion and micronutrients.

    Meal structure

    Breakfast / snack
    • Carb source
    • Protein source
    • Fruit
    • Optional small fat source

    Examples: oats + yogurt + banana, or tostadas + ham + cheese + fruit.

    Lunch / dinner
    • Main carb source
    • Main protein source
    • One or two easy vegetables when they fit the meal
    • Measured olive oil if the meat is lean

    Examples: rice + chicken + broccoli, pasta + lean ground beef + tomato sauce, or beef + potatoes + zucchini.

    Portion guide

    Carbs per main meal
    • Rice: 100 g raw
    • Pasta: 100 g raw
    • Potato: 300-350 g raw
    • Sweet potato: 300-350 g raw
    • Tortillas: 2 medium tortillas
    Protein per main meal
    • Chicken breast: 240-250 g raw
    • Lean beef: 240-250 g raw
    • Pork loin: 240-250 g raw
    • Ground beef: 250 g raw; reduce added oil if not very lean
    • Tuna: 1 can for snacks, 2 cans if used as a main protein

    Vegetables

    Aim for about 150 g of vegetables with main meals, but do not force them into combinations that feel bad. Good easy choices: broccoli, cauliflower, carrot, zucchini, onion, pumpkin, spinach and lettuce. Frozen broccoli or frozen mixed vegetables are completely fine.

    Olive oil rule

    • Lean chicken or very lean beef: 1 tablespoon olive oil is fine.
    • Regular ground beef: 0 added oil is usually better.
    • Moderately lean ground beef: 1 teaspoon if needed.

    Recipe handbook

    All recipes below use ingredients that are easy to get in Argentina and are built to fit the plan. Macros are approximate and can vary by brand and fat percentage of meat. Click any recipe to expand it.

    Lunch / dinner

    1. Chicken tortilla wrap with lettuce, carrot and cheese

    lunch / dinner 635 kcal · 56P · 47C · 22F
    Ingredients
    • 250 g chicken breast, raw
    • 2 wheat tortillas / rapiditas
    • 50 g lettuce, shredded
    • 60 g grated carrot
    • 30 g port salut light or mozzarella
    • 10 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, paprika
    Instructions
    1. Cut the chicken into strips and season with salt, pepper and paprika.
    2. Cook in a pan with the olive oil until done.
    3. Warm the tortillas for 20-30 seconds per side.
    4. Fill with lettuce, carrot, chicken and cheese.
    5. Wrap tightly and serve.

    2. Pasta with ground beef tomato sauce

    lunch / dinner 760 kcal · 49P · 82C · 24F
    Ingredients
    • 100 g dry pasta
    • 250 g lean ground beef
    • 200 g tomato passata or pure de tomate
    • 80 g onion, finely chopped
    • 5 ml olive oil
    • 1 clove garlic, chopped
    • Salt, pepper, oregano
    Instructions
    1. Boil the pasta in salted water until cooked.
    2. Cook onion and garlic in the olive oil for 2-3 minutes.
    3. Add the ground beef and break it up well.
    4. When browned, add passata, salt, pepper and oregano.
    5. Simmer 8-10 minutes and serve over the pasta.

    3. Chili con carne with rice

    lunch / dinner 790 kcal · 50P · 84C · 25F
    Ingredients
    • 80 g raw rice
    • 220 g lean ground beef
    • 130 g cooked red beans
    • 150 g tomato passata
    • 80 g onion, chopped
    • 50 g red bell pepper, chopped
    • 5 ml olive oil
    • Salt, paprika, cumin, aji molido
    Instructions
    1. Cook the rice separately.
    2. Cook onion and bell pepper in oil until soft.
    3. Add the ground beef and brown it.
    4. Add beans, passata and spices, then simmer 10 minutes.
    5. Serve the chili with the rice.

    4. Chicken rice bowl with broccoli and carrot

    lunch / dinner 700 kcal · 57P · 83C · 14F

    • 100 g broccoli florets
    • 80 g carrot, sliced
    • 10 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, garlic powder

    3. Cook chicken pieces in a pan with olive oil and seasonings.
    4. Serve everything in a bowl and add lemon juice if desired.

    5. Lean beef with potatoes and carrot-zucchini saute

    lunch / dinner 735 kcal · 51P · 66C · 28F
    Ingredients
    • 250 g lean beef steak or cubes
    • 350 g potatoes
    • 100 g zucchini, sliced
    • 70 g carrot, sliced thin
    • 10 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, oregano
    Instructions
    1. Cube the potatoes and boil or bake until cooked.
    2. Season and cook the beef in a hot pan.
    3. Saute zucchini and carrot in a pan until just tender.
    4. Serve everything together and add olive oil where needed.

    16. Breakfast burrito with egg, ham and cheese

    breakfast 520 kcal · 35P · 40C · 23F
    Ingredients
    • 2 rapiditas integrales
    • 2 eggs
    • 60 g jamon cocido
    • 30 g port salut light or mozzarella
    • 5 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, oregano
    • Optional: 1 cda palta, tomato slices
    Instructions

    2. Warm the rapiditas in the same pan for 20 seconds per side.
    3. Layer ham, scrambled eggs and cheese on each rapidita.
    4. Add palta or tomato if using.

    17. Anabolic french toast

    breakfast / pre-gym 660 kcal · 49P · 80C · 15F
    Ingredients
    • 4 slices pan lactal integral
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 scoop whey protein (30 g)
    • 50 ml leche descremada
    • Cinnamon
    • Edulcorante to taste
    • 1 banana, sliced (topping)
    • Optional: 1 cdita pasta de mani (topping)
    Instructions
    1. Whisk together the eggs, whey protein, milk, cinnamon and sweetener into a smooth batter.
    2. Dip each bread slice into the batter, pressing lightly so it absorbs well on both sides.
    3. Cook in a non-stick pan over medium heat, about 2 minutes per side until golden.
    4. Top with sliced banana and peanut butter if using.

    Adapted from Greg Doucette's recipe for Argentina: no sugar-free syrup needed, use banana and edulcorante instead. The whey in the batter is what makes it high-protein.

    18. Overnight proats

    breakfast / pre-gym 580 kcal · 41P · 80C · 11F
    Ingredients
    • 60 g oats
    • 1 scoop whey protein (30 g)
    • 200 ml leche descremada
    • 1 banana
    • 10 g pasta de mani
    • Cinnamon
    Instructions
    1. Mix oats, whey protein and milk in a jar or container.
    2. Stir well so there are no clumps, then cover and refrigerate overnight.
    3. In the morning, stir again, then top with sliced banana and peanut butter.
    4. Add cinnamon. Eat cold or microwave 1 minute if you prefer warm.

    Zero cooking, prepare the night before. Great for mornings before BJJ privates when time is tight.

    19. Protein smoothie (licuado proteico)

    breakfast / pre-gym 610 kcal · 42P · 81C · 13F
    Ingredients
    • 60 g oats
    • 1 scoop whey protein (30 g)
    • 200 ml leche descremada
    • 1 banana
    • 15 g pasta de mani
    • Ice cubes, optional
    • Cinnamon, optional
    Instructions
    1. Put everything in a blender: oats, whey, milk, banana, peanut butter and ice.
    2. Blend until completely smooth, about 30 seconds.
    3. Pour into a large glass or shaker bottle and drink.

    Drinkable breakfast in under a minute. The oats blend down completely if you use a decent blender. Good option when you cannot sit down to eat.

    22. Microwave oat mug cake

    breakfast / snack 520 kcal · 39P · 69C · 11F
    Ingredients
    • 60 g oats
    • 1 egg
    • 1 scoop whey protein (30 g)
    • 1 banana
    • 5 g baking powder (polvo de hornear)
    • Cinnamon
    • Edulcorante to taste
    Instructions
    1. Mash the banana in a microwave-safe mug or bowl.
    2. Add oats, egg, whey, baking powder, cinnamon and sweetener. Mix well.
    3. Microwave on high for 2-3 minutes until the cake is set and springs back when touched.
    4. Flip onto a plate or eat straight from the mug.

    Ready in under 5 minutes, no pan needed. Tastes like banana bread. Add a spoonful of pasta de mani on top if you want extra calories.

    23. Poached eggs on toast with avocado

    breakfast 440 kcal · 22P · 32C · 24F
    Ingredients
    • 2 eggs
    • 2 slices pan lactal integral
    • 1 cda palta (about 25 g)
    • 1 cdita vinagre blanco
    • Salt, pepper, aji molido
    • 1 fruit (apple, orange or banana)
    Instructions
    1. Fill a medium pot with water about 8 cm deep and bring to a boil, then lower to a bare simmer (tiny bubbles, no rolling boil).
    2. Add the vinegar to the water. Crack each egg into a small bowl first so you can slide it in gently.
    3. Create a gentle swirl in the water with a spoon and slide the egg into the center. The swirl helps the white wrap around the yolk.
    4. Cook for 3 minutes without touching. The white should be set and the yolk still runny.
    5. Lift out with a slotted spoon and rest on paper towel to drain. Repeat for the second egg.
    6. Toast the bread, spread the palta on top, and place the poached eggs on it.
    7. Season with salt, pepper and aji molido. Eat with the fruit on the side.

    The vinegar helps the white set faster so it doesn't spread. Use the freshest eggs you can find -- older eggs have thinner whites that spread more. If the first one looks messy, the second will be better.

    24. Soft-boiled eggs with toast soldiers

    breakfast 400 kcal · 22P · 32C · 19F
    Ingredients
    • 2 eggs
    • 2 slices pan lactal integral
    • 5 ml olive oil or a small knob of butter
    • Salt, pepper
    • 1 fruit (apple, orange or banana)
    Instructions
    1. Bring a small pot of water to a full boil.
    2. Gently lower the eggs in with a spoon (straight from the fridge is fine).
    3. Boil for exactly 6 minutes for a creamy, runny yolk. Use 7 minutes if you prefer the yolk just barely set.
    4. Transfer immediately to a bowl of cold water or run under the tap for 30 seconds to stop cooking.
    5. Toast the bread and cut into strips (soldiers). Spread with a thin layer of butter or olive oil.
    6. Tap the top off each egg with a knife, place in egg cups or small bowls, and dip the soldiers in.

    Timing matters: start the timer when the eggs go in, not when the water boils again. At 6 minutes the yolk is like a thick sauce -- perfect for dipping.

    25. Spinach and cheese omelette with toast

    breakfast 490 kcal · 32P · 30C · 26F
    Ingredients
    • 3 eggs
    • 40 g fresh spinach (or 30 g frozen, thawed and squeezed dry)
    • 30 g port salut light or mozzarella, diced or grated
    • 2 slices pan lactal integral
    • 5 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, oregano
    Instructions
    1. Beat the eggs with salt, pepper and oregano until just combined (don't over-beat).
    2. Heat the olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
    3. Pour in the eggs. Let them sit for 30 seconds until the bottom starts to set.
    4. Gently push the cooked edges toward the center with a spatula, tilting the pan so uncooked egg flows to the edges. Repeat 2-3 times.
    5. When the top is still slightly wet but mostly set, scatter the spinach and cheese on one half.
    6. Fold the other half over the filling. Cook 30 more seconds, then slide onto a plate.
    7. Toast the bread and serve alongside.

    The key to a good omelette is medium heat and not touching it too much. If you stir constantly you get scrambled eggs. If frozen spinach is all you have, squeeze it well or it will make the omelette watery.

    26. Shakshuka (eggs in tomato sauce) with bread

    breakfast 480 kcal · 25P · 42C · 22F
    Ingredients
    • 3 eggs
    • 200 g tomato passata or pure de tomate
    • 60 g onion, diced
    • 50 g red bell pepper (morron), diced
    • 1 clove garlic, chopped
    • 5 ml olive oil
    • Salt, pepper, pimenton dulce, comino
    • 2 slices bread for dipping
    Instructions
    1. Heat the olive oil in a medium pan over medium heat. Cook the onion and bell pepper for 3-4 minutes until soft.
    2. Add the garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
    3. Pour in the tomato passata. Add salt, pepper, pimenton and comino. Stir and simmer for 5 minutes until thickened slightly.
    4. Make 3 small wells in the sauce with a spoon and crack one egg into each.
    5. Cover the pan with a lid (or a plate) and cook on low heat for 6-8 minutes until the whites are fully set but the yolks are still soft.
    6. Toast or warm the bread. Serve the shakshuka in the pan and dip the bread into the sauce and yolks.

    This is a weekend-friendly breakfast -- takes a bit longer but makes one pan for everything. Leftovers of the sauce base keep well in the fridge for a day, so you can pre-make the sauce and just crack eggs into it next morning.
